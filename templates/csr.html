Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) is a business approach that integrates social and environmental concerns into the company's operations and interactions with stakeholders. It encompasses various initiatives and activities undertaken by businesses to ensure they are accountable not only for their economic performance but also for their impact on society and the environment. CSR initiatives often include philanthropy, ethical labor practices, environmental sustainability efforts, and community engagement. By embracing CSR, companies strive to contribute positively to the well-being of society, reduce their environmental footprint, and build trust with customers, employees, investors, and communities. CSR is increasingly seen as a fundamental aspect of responsible business conduct, with many organizations recognizing its importance in fostering long-term sustainability and resilience in an ever-changing global landscape.
